I see those "What We Eat in a Week" posts where every meal looks like it was styled by a food photographer and the mom is making sourdough at 6 AM while her kids peacefully do watercolors. That's not us. That has never been us.

Here's what we actually ate last week. A family of three — me, my husband, and our toddler. In Arizona. Where it's already too hot to want to cook most dinners.

## Monday

**Breakfast:** Scrambled eggs with cheese, sliced banana, toast. My son ate the banana and ignored the eggs. Classic.

**Lunch:** PB&J on [Dave's Killer Bread](https://amzn.to/3YwKj1R?tag=violetmama-20), apple slices, string cheese. Nothing revolutionary. Got the job done.

**Dinner:** Slow cooker chicken tacos. I threw chicken thighs in the [Crock-Pot](https://amzn.to/3YxMn2P?tag=violetmama-20) with salsa at noon and shredded it at 5. Corn tortillas, avocado, shredded cheese. Frozen corn on the side because I was not chopping vegetables at that point in the day.

**Snacks:** [Energy balls](https://amzn.to/4mRy7bP?tag=violetmama-20) from Sunday's batch. Goldfish crackers. Let's be honest.

## Tuesday

**Breakfast:** Oatmeal with [honey](https://amzn.to/4psWONx?tag=violetmama-20) and frozen blueberries. I use [Bob's Red Mill oats](https://amzn.to/4mRy7bP?tag=violetmama-20) and make it on the stove because instant oatmeal has a weird texture. This is a hill I will die on.

**Lunch:** Leftovers from taco night, deconstructed into a "taco bowl" for the toddler. He ate the cheese and avocado. Progress.

**Dinner:** Pasta with jarred marinara and steamed broccoli. My husband made this because I was finishing a code review and lost track of time. [Rao's marinara](https://amzn.to/3KcPqT1?tag=violetmama-20) is the one jarred sauce I don't feel weird about — the ingredient list is actually normal.

**Snacks:** Hummus and carrots. Raisins. A handful of my hidden chocolate stash.

## Wednesday

**Breakfast:** Smoothies — frozen banana, frozen spinach, [almond butter](https://amzn.to/45PXtke?tag=violetmama-20), milk, a little honey. My son calls this "green juice" and I let him because it sounds healthier than "a smoothie with enough honey to make him drink spinach."

**Lunch:** Quesadillas with black beans and cheese. Made in the [cast iron skillet](https://amzn.to/3K9vHn4?tag=violetmama-20) because everything is better in cast iron. Salsa and sour cream on the side.

**Dinner:** Sheet pan sausage and veggies. [Aidells chicken apple sausage](https://amzn.to/4mTpR3Q?tag=violetmama-20), sweet potatoes, and a bag of frozen green beans because I forgot to buy fresh ones. Olive oil, salt, pepper, 400°F for 25 minutes. This is the dinner I make when I have zero creative energy but want to feel like I tried.

**Snacks:** Apple with peanut butter. Graham crackers.

## Thursday

**Breakfast:** Yogurt bowls — plain [Stonyfield whole milk yogurt](https://amzn.to/4mVxK9L?tag=violetmama-20) with granola and sliced strawberries. I buy plain and add our own honey because kids' yogurt has an absurd amount of sugar.

**Lunch:** We went to Costco. Lunch was samples and a hot dog. I'm not going to pretend this didn't happen.

**Dinner:** Homemade pizza on [store-bought naan](https://amzn.to/3YzNp4R?tag=violetmama-20) because I do not have the bandwidth for pizza dough on a Thursday. Marinara, mozzarella, whatever veggies were in the fridge. My son picked off the peppers and ate plain cheese naan. Fine.

**Snacks:** Banana. Cheese cubes. More energy balls.

## Friday

**Breakfast:** Pancakes from scratch — flour, egg, milk, butter, baking powder. Takes maybe 10 minutes and my son helps stir. We do this most Fridays as an end-of-school-week thing.

**Lunch:** Chicken salad sandwiches using a rotisserie chicken I grabbed Thursday. Mayo, celery, salt, pepper. On Dave's Killer Bread again because we go through that stuff fast.

**Dinner:** Takeout. Friday night is pizza or Thai food from our local spot. No guilt. This is how we've stayed married through toddlerhood.

**Snacks:** Popcorn made on the stove with [coconut oil](https://amzn.to/3KbRt7M?tag=violetmama-20) and real butter. This is our Friday movie night thing and it's sacred.

## Saturday

**Breakfast:** Eggs and [turkey bacon](https://amzn.to/4mWnL5K?tag=violetmama-20). Toast. My husband cooks weekend breakfasts. I drink coffee and try not to talk to anyone for 30 minutes.

**Lunch:** Grilled cheese and tomato soup — [Amy's organic](https://amzn.to/3KdQr8N?tag=violetmama-20) from a can. Sometimes homemade is just opening a can of something decent.

**Dinner:** Grilled chicken, rice, and roasted zucchini. My husband grills and I make the sides. This is our teamwork dinner and it usually goes well unless the rice cooker decides to be difficult.

**Snacks:** Trail mix. Watermelon. Popsicles we made from blended fruit and yogurt using [these molds](https://amzn.to/3KfSt9P?tag=violetmama-20).

## Sunday

**Breakfast:** Biscuits from a can. Fruit. I know, I know. But sometimes a tube of Pillsbury is self-care.

**Lunch:** Big salad with leftover grilled chicken, whatever veggies are left, and [this dressing](https://amzn.to/3KhUv1R?tag=violetmama-20) we like from Primal Kitchen. Actually filling and cleans out the fridge.

**Dinner:** Soup night — usually a big pot of chicken and vegetable soup or [lentil soup](https://amzn.to/3KjWx3T?tag=violetmama-20) that makes enough for lunches early in the week. I use the [Dutch oven](https://amzn.to/3KlYz5V?tag=violetmama-20) and just throw things in.

**Snacks:** Made a new batch of energy balls for the week. My son "helped" which means he ate chocolate chips straight from the bag.

## The Honest Summary

Out of 21 meals, maybe 5 were genuinely "from scratch." Several involved frozen vegetables, canned soup, jarred sauce, or store-bought bread. One lunch was literally Costco samples. Friday dinner was takeout.

And you know what? Everyone was fed. Most of it was reasonably nutritious. Nobody cried at the table more than twice.

I'm not an influencer with a meal prep system and matching glass containers. I'm a mom who codes, homeschools, and tries to keep the food more real than processed most of the time. That's the bar. We clear it most weeks.

If your week looks like this — frozen veggies, repeat meals, the occasional "lunch was snacks" situation — you're doing fine. Better than fine. You're feeding your family actual food without losing your mind, and that counts.
